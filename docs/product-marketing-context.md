The North Capital terminal surfaces raw DLD data as-is, then layers a verdict on top. The data is the same data institutional buyers use. The terminal makes it accessible to any investor.

**How the terminal feeds the funnel:** Users arrive via organic search (specific project names, area names, DLD data queries), validate the data quality, and encounter the advisory CTA in context — when they are already thinking analytically about a purchase decision. This is a much higher-intent entry point than a cold listing page.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** The Dubai real estate market is systemically noisy. Almost every property is marketed as a "masterpiece" or "lifestyle investment." Developers and brokers are incentivized to close transactions, not to optimize for the investor's long-term outcome. Serious investors — especially those coming from finance or tech backgrounds — have no institutional-quality filter they can trust.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Traditional Dubai brokerages are volume-driven; advisors earn more from closing fast than from selecting carefully
- Wealth management firms (banks, family offices) understand portfolio theory but lack ground-level Dubai market specifics — supply pipeline by community, service charge benchmarks, distress deal identification
- PropTech apps and listing platforms provide data but no verdict; the investor still has to make the analytical call alone
- Developer sales teams are structurally conflicted — they cannot tell you to pass on a deal

**What it costs them:**
- Selecting oversupplied communities with yield compression and falling PSF
- Paying service charges that erode net yield (some communities charge 3–4x the DLD benchmark)
- Missing off-plan entry windows for projects with strong post-handover upside
- Buying at retail pricing when distressed inventory is available at 10–20% below DLD benchmark

**Emotional tension:** Fear of making a bad investment in what feels like a speculative market. Distrust of anyone in Dubai real estate who seems too enthusiastic. Anxiety about cross-border complexity (mortgages, currency moves, property management from abroad). Stress from not having a trusted advisor who will tell them when to pass.

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors — Traditional Dubai Real Estate Brokerages**
Same problem (connecting investors to Dubai property), different approach. Fall short because: sales-volume focus, no analytical filter, no ability to tell clients to pass on a deal, no DLD-level data surfaced to the client.

**Secondary competitors — Wealth Management Firms**
Different solution (portfolio-level wealth management) for overlapping audience. Fall short because: miss Dubai-specific supply-side dynamics, service charge benchmarks, and off-plan pricing intelligence. Generic real estate allocation advice, not asset-level due diligence.

**Indirect competitors — Self-investing via PropTech apps (Property Finder, Bayut, Dubizzle)**
Conflicting approach. The investor attempts to replicate the advisory layer themselves using listing platforms. Falls short because: data is curated by listing agents, not DLD; no benchmark pricing; no off-market access; no institutional verdict on whether to proceed.
