## The Three Buyer Archetypes

Every inbound lead belongs to one of three archetypes. Identifying which one early determines everything: the product, the area, the price point, the urgency, and the data narrative you use.

### Archetype A — Golden Visa Buyer (AED 2M+ threshold)
- Primary motivation: UAE residency for self and/or family, not necessarily yield
- Key signal: mentions visa, residency, schooling, or family relocation
- Price sensitivity: lower — they need to clear AED 2M, so they anchor at that level
- Timeline: often urgent — visa application tied to purchase completion
- DLD data angle: transaction comps in their target area to confirm they are above the AED 2M threshold cleanly; avoid communities where AED 2M gets a unit that is borderline

### Archetype B — Yield Investor (local or overseas, rental return focus)
- Primary motivation: ROI, passive income, portfolio diversification
- Key signal: asks about gross yield, occupancy rates, tenant profile, or payment plans
- Price sensitivity: moderate — will trade location for better yield metrics
- Timeline: flexible unless they have capital sitting idle
- DLD data angle: historical transaction volumes and price index trends in target area; service charge exposure as a yield killer; supply pipeline risk (oversupply in 18-36M horizon)

### Archetype C — End-User / Self-Occupier (expat or relocating)
- Primary motivation: lifestyle, commute, school catchment, community feel
- Key signal: asks about amenities, parking, floor level, nearby dining
- Price sensitivity: higher — often comparing rent vs. buy math
- Timeline: often tied to lease expiry or school year
- DLD data angle: price index trajectory (are they buying at a local peak or into a rising market?); comparable unit sizes vs. asking price

Misidentifying the archetype — or failing to establish it — leads to showing the wrong product, using the wrong data narrative, and losing the deal to an advisor who listened.

## Call Structure: The Advisory Discovery Call

### Opening (2 minutes): Set the Upfront Contract

The upfront contract eliminates ambiguity and signals that this is a data-led, honest advisory conversation — not a high-pressure sales pitch.

```
"Thanks for reaching out. I want to make the most of our time together, so
here is what I had in mind:

I will ask you a few questions about what you are looking for and what
matters most to you — so I can point you at the right opportunities rather
than just flood you with listings. You ask me anything you want; I will
give you straight answers backed by transaction data from the DLD registry.

By the end of this call, one of three things will happen: we will see a
clear fit and I will put together a shortlist for you, we will realise the
market does not have what you need right now and I will tell you that
honestly, or we will need more information before we can narrow it down.

Any of those is fine. Does that work for you?"
```

This positions the broker as a data-led advisor, not a listing pusher. It also gives explicit permission to deliver an honest "not yet" — which builds more credibility than a forced viewing.

### Discovery Phase (18–20 minutes): Map the Buyer Situation

Spend the majority of the call here. You are not ready to recommend a community or unit until you can articulate the buyer's situation back to them more clearly than they described it.

**Territory opener (choose by context):**

- Inbound: "What prompted you to reach out today?" → Let them set the frame.
- Referral: "I know [referrer] mentioned you were thinking about property in Dubai — where are you at with that?"
- Portal lead: "You enquired about [area/project] — what drew you to that one specifically?"

Then listen for the archetype signal before asking anything else.

---

#### Section 1: Intent and Situation

**Establish why now:**
- "Is this your first property in Dubai, or do you already own here?"
- "What has changed recently that has made this feel like the right time?"
- "Are you currently renting in Dubai, or are you looking at this as an investment from overseas?"

**Establish the primary driver (this is the archetype question):**
- "Is the goal to live in the property, generate rental income, or secure residency — or some combination?"
- "When you think about what a successful purchase looks like for you 12 months from now, what does that picture look like?"

**If Golden Visa signals appear:**
- "Is the residency component the primary driver, or is it more of a bonus on top of an investment you were planning anyway?"
- "Are you looking to bring family onto the visa, and do you have a timeline tied to that?"
- "Are you clear on the AED 2M threshold requirement, and is that a comfortable level for you or are we working right at the edge of it?"

**If yield signals appear:**
- "What gross yield are you benchmarking against — is there a number that would make this feel like a good decision?"
- "Are you comparing this to returns in another market, or is Dubai the primary focus?"
- "Are you thinking furnished short-term rental, or unfurnished long-term tenancy?"

**If end-user signals appear:**
- "What does your day-to-day routine look like — where do you work, where do the kids go to school, where do you spend your weekends?"
- "Are you currently renting somewhere you like, or is the current situation part of what's driving this?"
- "What would make you regret the purchase in year two — what is your biggest concern?"

---

#### Section 2: Area, Budget, and Timeline

**Area preference — go beyond the obvious:**
- "When you say [area], what is it specifically that appeals? Is it the community, the commute, the price point, or something else?"
- "Have you visited the areas you are considering, or is this based on what you have seen online?"
- "Are there areas you have ruled out, and if so, why?"

*Use this to introduce DLD data:*
- "Interesting — that area has seen [X]% transaction volume growth over the past 12 months according to DLD data. Have you looked at how that compares to [adjacent area], which has a lower entry price but similar yield profile?"

**Budget:**
- "Do you have a range in mind, or are you still calibrating based on what the market looks like?"
- "Are you planning to pay cash, use a mortgage, or a developer payment plan?"
- "If you are financing, have you spoken to a bank yet, or would it help if I connected you with a mortgage broker before we look at anything?"

**Timeline:**
- "Is there a date you are working towards — a lease expiry, a visa application window, a school term start?"
- "If you found the right unit tomorrow, how quickly could you move?"
- "What would slow you down — is there a decision-maker we should include in these conversations?"

---

#### Section 3: DLD Data Qualification Questions

This is what separates a data-led advisor from a listing-pusher. Use these to surface real risk and real opportunity — and to demonstrate that you have done the work.

**Transaction history:**
- "I pulled the DLD transaction history for [target community]. There were [X] transactions in the past 12 months at an average of AED [Y] per sq ft. Does that align with what you have been seeing, or does it change how you are thinking about value?"
- "One thing worth knowing: the number of distressed sales in this community over the past 6 months is [low/elevated]. That tells me [buyers are holding / there is some motivated seller opportunity]. Does that matter to you?"

**Service charge exposure:**
- "Service charges in [community] are running at AED [X] per sq ft per year, which is [above/below] the Dubai average. For a [Y] sq ft unit, that is AED [Z] per year before maintenance. Is that something you have factored into your yield calculation?"
- "This is one of the areas where buyers get surprised after completion. I can show you the RERA service charge register for any community you are considering."

**Supply pipeline risk:**
- "The supply pipeline for [area] shows [X] units completing in the next 18–24 months. In a tight-supply environment that is fine; in an oversupply environment it puts pressure on resale values and rental rates. Do you want me to walk you through what the data shows for this community?"
- "If you are buying off-plan, the question I always ask is: what does the area look like in 24 months when keys hand over? That is when you are competing with every other unit in the same building for the same tenant."

**Price index:**
- "The DLD residential price index for [area/property type] has moved [X]% over the past [Y] years. If you are buying for capital appreciation, are you comfortable with where we are in the cycle, or would you like to walk through the data together?"

### Red Flag Signals

These are patterns that indicate the deal will not close, or will close badly, without intervention.

| Signal | What It Usually Means | Response |
|--------|----------------------|----------|
| "I am just exploring" | No timeline, no urgency — may need 6+ months | Qualify timeline explicitly: "What would need to be true for you to move in the next 90 days?" |
| "Send me some listings" | No trust established, comparing across 5 brokers | Do not send listings. Offer a curated shortlist after this call to differentiate. |
| "My friend told me to look at [area]" | No independent view of the market | Use DLD data to anchor their thinking independently before they see units. |
| Cannot articulate primary driver | Has not done the thinking yet | Slow down. Run the archetype questions again. Do not pitch until the driver is clear. |
| "We need to discuss as a family" | A decision-maker is not on the call | Identify who else needs to be involved and schedule a follow-up that includes them. |
| Budget is right at the AED 2M threshold | Golden Visa buyers at the edge have higher risk of falling through | Confirm the exact unit price required for the visa to be valid; do not assume. |
| "I bought off-plan in [problematic project]" | Has prior negative experience | Acknowledge it explicitly. Do not dismiss it. Use DLD data to distinguish the current opportunity from their prior experience. |
| No mortgage pre-approval, financing buyer | Deal will stall at offer stage | Introduce a mortgage broker before recommending a unit. |

### Tailored Recommendation (6 minutes): Only What Is Relevant

After — and only after — you have mapped the buyer's situation, present a shortlist mapped directly to what they told you.

```
"Based on what you have shared — [restate their driver, area preference, budget,
and timeline in their words] — here are two or three options that I think
genuinely fit, and I will tell you why."
```

For each option:
1. Why it fits their stated criteria
2. One honest trade-off or risk to be aware of
3. The DLD data point that supports or complicates the recommendation

Do not show everything available. Do not forward a generic portal search. Relevance and honesty beat volume.

---

### Next Steps (3–4 minutes): Be Explicit

- Define exactly what happens next: "I will send you the shortlist by [day], and I will include the DLD transaction comps and service charge data for each unit."
- If a viewing is the next step: "I will arrange access. Before we go, I recommend you also speak to a mortgage broker — do you want me to make that introduction, or do you have one?"
- Identify who else needs to be involved: "You mentioned your partner needs to be part of the decision — can we find a time when you are both available for the next call?"
- Set the next contact point before ending the call.
- Agree on what a "no" looks like: "If after the viewing it does not feel right, just tell me — I would rather know so we can recalibrate than have you go quiet on me."

## Objection Handling: The AECR Framework

Objections are diagnostic information. They tell you what the buyer is actually thinking.

**Acknowledge** — Validate the concern without agreeing or arguing
- "That is a completely fair point."

**Empathize** — Show you understand why they feel that way
- "If I had seen the market correction in 2008 or 2020, I would be cautious too."

**Clarify** — Ask a question to understand the real concern behind the stated one
- "When you say the timing does not feel right, is that about the market cycle, a personal finance question, or something else?"

**Reframe** — Offer a new perspective grounded in data, not opinion
- "What I can show you is what the DLD transaction data says about [area] over the past 24 months — and let you form your own view."

### Common Objections in Dubai Property

| Objection | What It Usually Means | Data-Led Response |
|-----------|----------------------|-------------------|
| "The market is too hot right now" | Fear of buying at the peak | Show price index trend; compare to prior peaks; discuss supply pipeline as forward indicator |
| "I want to wait and see" | No urgency established | Ask: "What would need to be true in the market for you to feel confident?" Then show the data against that answer |
| "I can get a better deal off-plan" | Comparing cash flow, not total return | Walk through off-plan vs. ready: capital at risk during construction, service charge exposure from day one, rental income delay |
| "Another broker showed me something cheaper" | Comparing different products or misrepresented comps | Pull DLD transaction comps for that specific building or community to anchor the conversation in real data |
| "The developer has a great payment plan" | Being led by financing structure, not the underlying asset | Ask about post-handover price per sq ft vs. comparable ready units; discuss supply concentration risk |
| "I am worried about the visa rules changing" | Regulatory uncertainty | Acknowledge it; note the AED 2M threshold has been stable since 2022; do not overstate certainty |

## What Great Discovery Looks Like in Property Advisory

**Signs you nailed it:**
- The buyer says "I had not thought about service charges that way" — you educated them on real cost
- The buyer reveals their actual budget is higher than the number they led with
- The buyer asks "Which of those two areas would you buy in?" — they trust your view
- You can summarise their situation back to them and they say "Exactly, that is it"
- The buyer asks to introduce their partner or family member to the next call

**Signs you rushed it:**
- You are recommending communities before you know the primary driver (visa, yield, lifestyle)
- The buyer is asking you about areas rather than you guiding them
- You do not know whether they are financing or paying cash
- You do not know their timeline or what is creating urgency (or the absence of it)
- You sent listings before this call was done

## Coaching Principles

- **Discovery is not interrogation.** It is helping the buyer see their own situation more clearly, including things they have not fully thought through — like service charge exposure or supply pipeline risk in their target community. If the buyer feels interrogated, you are asking questions without providing value in return.
- **Silence is a tool.** After asking about primary motivation or budget, wait. The first answer is the surface answer. The answer after the pause is the real one.
- **The best advisors talk less.** The buyer should talk 60% of the time. If you are talking more than 40%, you are pitching listings, not discovering needs.
- **Data earns trust faster than enthusiasm.** A broker who says "let me pull the DLD transaction history for that building" in the first 10 minutes of a call signals a completely different advisory standard than one who says "it is a great community."
- **Qualify out fast.** A buyer with no clear driver, no budget clarity, and no timeline is not a deal. It is pipeline noise. Have the courage to say "I do not think now is the right time for you" — it builds more trust than a forced viewing.
- **Never ask a question you could have researched.** "What is your budget range?" is fine. "What areas are in Dubai?" is not. Know the DLD data for their stated areas of interest before the call.

---

## Communication Style for Coaching

- **Be Socratic**: Lead with questions, not prescriptions. "What happened when you asked about their timeline?" is better than "You should always ask about timeline."
- **Use call recordings as evidence**: "At 8 minutes you identified they were a yield investor. At 12 minutes you were recommending a waterfront community that has a 3.2% gross yield. Those two things do not fit."
- **Praise specific technique, not outcomes**: "The way you introduced the service charge data before recommending the community was excellent — you framed yourself as an advisor, not a salesperson."
- **Be honest about what is missing**: "You finished the call without knowing if they have mortgage pre-approval. That means you will get to offer stage and stall. Fix that in the follow-up before anything else." Direct, based on pattern recognition, never harsh.
